# Keuken-Centrum Utrecht Brand Repositioning Strategy

## Brand Intent

Keuken-Centrum Utrecht should feel like an established premium kitchen destination in Utrecht, not a standard retailer or generic showroom website. The brand signal must be immediate: German precision, Italian elegance, personal consultation, and expertise since 1978.

## Audit Summary

- The overall structure is strong, but several visual details still suggest a retailer mindset instead of a luxury showroom.
- Typography has been partially upgraded, but some local treatments still feel promotional rather than editorial.
- The new ivory, navy, green, and gold palette is in place, but legacy dark hero classes and older premium-template styles still exist in the global stylesheet.
- Spacing is stronger than before, though some CTA and link treatments still use uppercase or high-contrast gestures that feel less refined.
- The homepage narrative is now closer to a premium journey:
  hero -> partner credibility -> guided configurator -> style collections -> showroom visit -> final contact.

## Positioning Statement

De Premium Keukenbestemming van Utrecht.

## Brand Pillars

1. Since 1978 authority
2. German precision
3. Italian elegance
4. Personal showroom guidance
5. Premium residential expertise

## Design Direction

- Use light, calm, architectural backgrounds as the default canvas.
- Use dark surfaces strategically for authority sections, not as the entire website mood.
- Let typography, spacing, imagery, and composition create hierarchy before adding decoration.
- Avoid startup motion, SaaS cards, retail badges, loud gradients, and e-commerce density.

## Typography System

- Display and editorial headings: `Instrument Serif`
- Body copy: `Geist`
- Navigation: `Geist Medium`
- Buttons: `Geist Medium`
- Statistics and key numeric anchors: `Geist SemiBold`

Principles:

- Headings should feel editorial, quiet, and architectural.
- Body copy should stay restrained, clear, and premium.
- UI labels should avoid shouting unless used as subtle section markers.

## Color System

- Warm Ivory: `#F7F4EE`
- Deep Navy: `#07111B`
- Luxury Green: `#4F7A4D`
- Soft Green: `#6E9A69`
- Champagne Gold: `#C6A36B`
- Warm Gray: `#E7E2D9`
- Pure White: `#FFFFFF`

Principles:

- Ivory and white carry most of the page.
- Navy anchors premium contrast and authority.
- Green supports expertise and consultation, never retail loudness.
- Gold is reserved for refinement, emphasis, and detail accents.

## Spacing System

- Increase section rhythm to create pause and confidence.
- Use generous negative space around headings, imagery, and CTA groupings.
- Keep cards visually lighter and more breathable than before.
- Avoid compressed layouts that feel transactional.

## Section Strategy

### Hero

- Immediate premium statement.
- Large editorial heading.
- Strong left-side readability wash over imagery.
- Trust metrics integrated into the hero composition.

### Brands

- Communicate curated partner authority.
- Feel like selected premium houses under one roof, not logo inventory.

### Configurator

- Position as a preparation tool for a better consultation, not a generic form wizard.

### Collections

- Present as curated style worlds.
- Feel closer to an interior catalog than a product card grid.

### Showroom

- Sell the physical experience of materials, finishes, and personal advice.

### Final CTA and Footer

- Reinforce establishment, consultation quality, and local authority in Utrecht.

## Implementation Priorities

1. Remove legacy homepage CSS that no longer matches the active design language.
2. Normalize active homepage typography to the new serif/sans hierarchy.
3. Reduce uppercase retail cues where they weaken the editorial tone.
4. Align CTA, collection, and sticky conversion styling with the new token system.
5. Preserve responsiveness, accessibility, and restrained motion.

## Validation Standard

The site is successful when it no longer reads as a kitchen retailer and instead feels like a premium European showroom brand with architectural discipline and established trust.
